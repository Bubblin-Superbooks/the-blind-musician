Sometimes during the heat of noonday, when all around was quiet, when human life seemed at a standstill, and Nature had lapsed into that peculiar repose beneath which the noiseless current of life is felt rather than seen, the face of the blind boy likewise assumed an expression peculiar to himself. One who observed him at such moments might fancy that his vague thoughts had found an echo in his heart, like the uncertain melody of a song.
